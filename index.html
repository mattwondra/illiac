About

The ILLIAC Spring Festival takes place in Downtown Urbana, Illinois in May, as a hearty handshake to the students ready to depart for the Summer, and as a welcome wagon for the wonderful weather to come and the bounty of local produce just waiting to be consumed throughout the year.

Why is it called ILLIAC? We named it that to honor the fact that Urbana, Illinois was actually home to the first piece of computer music ever composed, The Illiac Suite, in 1956 at the University of Illinois. Champaign-Urbana is home to a very rich musical history, and there is something strong in the first three letters I-L-L that makes us all proud in one way or another.

So, that's why we went with ILLIAC. Nothing more, nothing less.

The event is produced by The Nicodemus Agency, known best for staging Pygmalion Music Festival for the past decade all around both Champaign and Urbana, and done so in partnership with The Canopy Club, the most prolific and important live music venue of the past 15 years in all of downstate Illinois.

Our sponsors are many, and growing, but this simply couldn't be possible without the support of the City of Urbana, the Urbana Business Association, and the Common Ground Food Co-op. There are others of course, so please click the PARTNERS link and thank them by patronizing their businesses on the day of the festival and throughout the year. But our Presenting Sponsors were the engine that drove this festival to reality. We thank them kindly.

What's it all about? It's real easy: this is a FREE, outdoor event that is designed to get people out on a Saturday to enjoy Downtown Urbana and all of its offerings. We'll supply the entertainment, the refreshments, and the sunshine, and you provide us with company.

Deal? Deal.

Who’s doing this?

The ILLIAC Spring Festival is run by The Nicodemus Agency and The Canopy Club. Between the two organizations, they have over 50 years between them living and working in Urbana, Illinois.
